Cancel, Refund, Pause & Reactivate Memberships

The four ways to end (or pause) a paid membership and what each one does

/admin/members → member detail · /admin/billing/orders

What this guide covers

The four ways an admin can end (or pause) a paid membership: cancel-now, cancel-at-renewal, refund (full or partial), and pause. Each has a different effect on the database, on Stripe, and on what email the member receives. Pick the right one for the situation.

2. Cancel now (immediate cancellation)

Business logic — what actually happens

  • cancelledAt set to now, cancellationReason stored, cancelledBy = your admin id.
  • paymentStatus flipped to CANCELLED.
  • cancelAtPeriodEnd = false (terminal cancel, not scheduled).
  • Profile membership_status recomputed via syncProfileMembershipStatus — typically goes to "expired" or "none" depending on whether other active memberships exist.
  • Audit log entry written: action = membership_cancel, with old/new values.
  • Admin notification fired (non-blocking) so other staff see the cancellation in their alerts.
  • No Stripe action is taken — if the subscription is recurring you must also cancel it through Stripe or use Refund (which handles both).
  • No email is sent to the member by default — they'll see the cancellation when they next visit their dashboard.

Click-through

1
Open the member detail
Navigate to /admin/members, search by name/email/MECA ID, click the row.
2
Find the membership card
Each active membership shows on the detail page with its status badge and an actions menu.
3
Choose "Cancel now"
A reason prompt requires at least 5 characters — this is recorded against the membership and in the audit log.
4
Confirm
The membership badge flips to Cancelled within a second; the member loses access immediately on their next request.

Cancel-now does NOT refund money

If the member is owed a refund, use the Refund flow instead — it cancels and refunds in one operation, and emails the member.

3. Cancel at renewal (end-of-period)

Business logic

  • cancelledAt = now, cancellationReason stored, cancelledBy = admin id.
  • cancelAtPeriodEnd = true.
  • paymentStatus stays PAID — the membership is still active.
  • The nightly cron deactivates it when endDate passes.
  • Auto-renew is disabled — the member won't be charged again.
  • Audit log: action = membership_cancel_at_renewal.

Click-through

1
Open member detail → membership card
Find the active membership.
2
Click "Cancel at renewal"
Provide reason (≥5 chars).
3
Confirm
A success message lists the exact deactivation date (the membership's endDate). The member retains full access until then.

4. Refund — full or partial

Business logic — full refund

  • If the membership has a stripePaymentIntentId, a Stripe refund is created for the full amount through the Stripe API.
  • Membership row: cancelledAt = now, cancellationReason prefixed with "REFUND: ", paymentStatus → REFUNDED.
  • Profile membership status recomputed.
  • Email sent — the member receives the "Membership Cancelled / Refunded" template with refund amount and reason.
  • Audit log: action = membership_refund.

Business logic — partial refund

  • You specify an amount in cents that's less than the total paid.
  • Stripe partial refund issued.
  • Membership stays active — paymentStatus remains PAID, cancelledAt stays null.
  • A note is appended to cancellationReason: Partial refund $XX.XX: <reason>. Multiple partial refunds stack.
  • Audit log: action = membership_refund_partial.

Stripe refund failure rolls everything back

If the Stripe refund call fails (insufficient funds, expired charge, network), the membership is not cancelled or modified. The error is surfaced to the admin UI; no audit entry is written, no email is sent. Retry once Stripe is healthy.

Click-through (full refund)

1
Open membership card
Member detail page, find the active or recently-cancelled membership.
2
Click "Refund"
Choose Full refund. Enter reason. Confirm — Stripe refund is issued and confirmation appears.
3
Verify
Member's billing history (member detail → Billing tab) shows the refund. Audit log shows membership_refund. Member's inbox should have the refund email within a minute.

Click-through (partial refund)

Same flow but choose Partial and enter the dollar amount. The membership remains active. Use this for goodwill credits ("waive this month") or correcting an overcharge.

5. Pause and resume a subscription

Business logic

  • Pause requires a stripeSubscriptionId on the membership — only members on recurring auto-renew can be paused.
  • Calls Stripe's pauseSubscription: no charge at the next cycle.
  • Membership row is not changed — paid status, end date, everything else stays.
  • Audit log: membership_pause.
  • Resume reverses it via Stripe; charges resume at the next cycle.

When to pause vs cancel-at-renewal

Pause = "I'll be back in 3 weeks, don't charge me but keep my account intact." Cancel-at-renewal = "I'm not renewing, end at period end." Pause is reversible mid-period; cancel-at-renewal is reversible up until the period actually ends.

6. Reactivate a cancelled-at-renewal membership

If a member changes their mind before their cancelled-at-renewal membership actually expires, you can reverse it.

1
Open the membership
It will show "Cancels at <endDate>" status.
2
Click Reactivate
cancelAtPeriodEnd is cleared, cancellationReason and cancelledBy are blanked. Auto-renew is re-enabled.
3
Verify Stripe
If the subscription was already cancelled at the Stripe end (rare for cancel-at-renewal, but possible if they were on an external subscription), the dev team needs to recreate it. Otherwise no Stripe action is needed.

7. Bulk actions across many members

The Members page (/admin/members) has a bulk-action toolbar that exposes the same operations across many rows at once. Tick rows, then:

  • Refund all — Stripe full refund + immediate cancel for every selected membership.
  • Cancel now — immediate cancel for every selected membership; no refund.
  • Cancel at renewal — schedule end-of-period cancel for every selected membership.

Each bulk action requires a reason of at least 5 characters and produces a per-row results panel showing succeeded vs failed. Failed rows show the underlying error (e.g. "already cancelled", "no Stripe payment intent").

Bulk refunds are irreversible

Triple-check the selection before confirming. Stripe refunds cannot be undone once they're issued.
