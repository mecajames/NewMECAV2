Member Activity Tracking

Per-member, per-page navigation history for logged-in members

/admin/member-activity · /admin/members → member detail → Activity tab

What this feature is

First-party tracking that records every page a logged-in member visits — which page, how long, what OS/browser/device, what referrer. Anonymous (non-member) traffic continues to flow through Google Analytics 4 only; this system records authenticated members exclusively. Members can opt out from their Profile → Privacy panel.

2. The per-member Activity tab

1
Open the member's detail page
From /admin/members click any member row. Their detail page loads with a tab bar at the top.
2
Click the Activity tab
Sits under the Competition tab group along with Event Registrations, Results, and Communications.
3
What you see
Three summary tiles (total page views, sessions, window) plus a list of sessions newest-first. Each session card shows the start time, page count, total time in session, OS / browser / device. Click "Show pages" to expand a session and see every page visited with timestamp, time on page, and referrer.
4
Use it for support
When a member says "I tried to register but it didn't work," open the Activity tab — you'll see the exact path they took, where they got stuck, and what device they were on. That's usually enough to reproduce the issue without lengthy back-and-forth.

Window: last 200 page views

The tab shows the most recent 200 page views by default. For members who use the platform heavily that's typically a few weeks of history. To go further back, use the cross-member dashboard or run a database query directly.

3. The cross-member dashboard

/admin/member-activity

Aggregates across the whole member base for a configurable window (24h / 7d / 30d / 90d). Useful when you want to know:

  • Which pages are most-visited by signed-in members (vs anonymous)?
  • Who's the most active member this week?
  • What OS / browser / device split do members use? (Drives where to focus mobile-vs-desktop work.)

What's on the page

  • Summary tiles — total page views, distinct members, distinct sessions, average time per page.
  • Top pages — top 25 pages with views and unique members.
  • Most active members — top 25 by view count, click-through to their detail page.
  • OS / Browser / Device breakdowns — bar charts of relative share.

4. Opt-out behavior

Members can disable tracking from their Profile → Privacy panel. The toggle writes profiles.analytics_opt_out = true. Once on:

  • The frontend tracker still fires (we don't want to leak the opt-out state to client-side JS), but the backend endpoint silently discards the page view.
  • The member's existing history is preserved unless they request deletion. The Activity tab on the admin side shows an "opted out" notice instead of fetching new data.
  • Opt-out is reversible — flipping the toggle back resumes tracking from that point forward.

Default state

Tracking is on by default for new members. The privacy policy discloses this and the toggle is prominent in the profile UI. Members who care about it will see it; members who don't will be tracked under the disclosed terms.

5. Privacy policy disclosure

The platform's public Privacy Policy was updated when this feature shipped to add a "Member Activity Tracking (signed-in members only)" section under 1. What Information Do We Collect? The disclosure includes:

  • The complete list of fields captured (matching the Tracked column above).
  • Why we collect it (operate and improve the platform, support, internal usage analysis).
  • Explicit "this is not sold and not used for cross-site advertising."
  • How to opt out (Profile → Privacy panel).
  • That existing records persist until deletion is requested.
  • Note that anonymous (non-member) traffic is handled separately by the third-party GA4 service.

If your jurisdiction or policy requirements change, edit apps/frontend/src/pages/PrivacyPolicyPage.tsx — the disclosure paragraph is searchable for "Member Activity Tracking."

6. Under the hood (technical)

LayerWhat happens
React RouteruseMemberPageTracking hook detects the location change and (only when authenticated) fires POST /api/analytics/track-page.
Session IDGenerated client-side, stored in sessionStorage under mecaSessionId — survives route changes, resets when the tab/window closes.
Backend endpointMemberAnalyticsController.trackPage() validates auth, then calls MemberAnalyticsService.trackPageView().
UA parsingua-parser-js extracts OS family, OS version, browser family, browser version, device type. Mobile/tablet inferred from device.type or os.name when device.type is missing (typical for desktops).
Duration backfillBefore inserting the new view, the service finds the most recent un-finalized view in the same session and sets its duration_ms = now - viewed_at. Capped at 30 minutes — anything longer is a user walking away.
Storagemember_page_views table. Cascade-deletes when the profile is deleted (FK ON DELETE CASCADE).
Indexes(user_id, viewed_at DESC) for the per-member query, (session_id, viewed_at DESC) for duration backfill, (page_path, viewed_at DESC) for top-pages reports.
