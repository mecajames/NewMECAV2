Analytics — Site, Search Console, Banner

Three dashboards, what each one tracks, and how to read the reports

/admin/analytics · /admin/search-console · /admin/seo-settings · /admin/banners/analytics

Three analytics surfaces

The platform exposes three different analytics dashboards, each answering a different question. Site Analytics tells you who's visiting and what they're reading. Search Console tells you how Google sees the site. Banner Analytics tells you which ads are working. This guide covers all three.

1. Site Analytics — pageviews, visitors, sessions

Where it comes from

The platform logs every page request to its own analytics tables (no third-party tracker). For each request we record: the URL path, the user id (if signed in) or anonymous session id, the referrer, user agent, IP-country, and timestamp.

Top-of-page tiles

  • Pageviews — total page renders in the selected period.
  • Unique visitors — distinct anonymous + logged-in users.
  • Sessions — visit clusters; a session ends after 30 minutes of inactivity.
  • Avg. session duration — useful comparative metric vs prior periods.

Charts and tables

  • Daily traffic chart — pageviews per day. Spot weekend dips, post-event spikes.
  • Top pages — top 20 by pageviews; click any to see referrer breakdown.
  • Top referrers — where your traffic comes from. Direct, search engines, partner sites.
  • Geographic breakdown — visitors by US state and top international countries.
  • Device split — mobile / tablet / desktop %.

Date filters

Last 7 days / Last 30 days / Last 90 days / Custom range / This year. Toggle "Compare to previous period" to see the delta against the equivalent prior window.

2. Search Console — Google traffic

This dashboard pulls directly from the Google Search Console API. For it to work an admin must have connected Search Console in Site Settings.

What you see

  • Total impressions — how many times pages of yours appeared in Google search results.
  • Total clicks — clicks from Google to the site.
  • Average CTR — click rate.
  • Average position — where you rank on average across all queries that surfaced your pages.
  • Top queries — what people searched to find you.
  • Top pages — which pages get the most search clicks.

Reading the report

  • Impressions trending up + position trending down = healthy SEO progress (more eyeballs, ranking higher).
  • Position dropping while impressions stay flat = competitor pressure on your existing keywords.
  • A "top query" with thousands of impressions but low CTR = title or description is misaligned. Edit the meta in /admin/seo-settings for that page.

3. Banner Analytics

Covered in detail in the Banner Ads guide. Quick summary:

  • Daily trend per banner.
  • CSV export for advertiser reporting.

4. Weekly digest emails

Every Sunday the system emails admins a digest summarizing the prior week:

  • Total revenue (memberships, shop, registrations).
  • New members signed up.
  • Renewals (succeeded vs failed).
  • Refunds issued.
  • Support tickets opened / resolved.
  • Top-traffic pages.

To preview the digest without waiting until Sunday, use /admin/email-testing → "Send Test Weekly Digest" to your own inbox.
