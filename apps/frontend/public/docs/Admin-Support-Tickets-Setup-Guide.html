Support Ticket System — Setup Guide

How to build and configure the dynamic support form, step by step

/admin/tickets → Setup Guide

Who this is for

This guide is written for MECA admins — including non-technical admins — who set up the support ticket system. It explains, in plain English, every tab on the /admin/tickets page and the exact order to configure them in. You do not need to write any code. If you can fill in a form, you can set this up.

The big idea: the support form asks the member one question at a time. They pick a Department → then a Category that belongs to that department → and then the form automatically shows any extra fields you've attached to that category. You control all of it from Support Tickets → Settings, where a sub-menu holds every configuration screen (Assignments, Departments, Categories, Custom Fields, Routing, General, Setup Guide) — no developer needed.

1. How the support form works (the big picture)

When a member (or a guest) opens "Create Support Ticket", they fill it out top to bottom. Each choice can reveal the next thing to fill in. Here is the flow:

Subject │ ▼ Department ◄── you create these in the "Departments" tab (e.g. Events, Billing, Membership) │ ▼ Category ◄── you create these in the "Categories" tab; each belongs to a department │ (e.g. under "Events": Points, Results, Staff) ▼ Extra fields ◄── you create these in the "Custom Fields" tab and attach them to categories │ (e.g. "Related Event", "My Purchase", "Staff Member", a dropdown, a date…) ▼ Description + Screenshots │ ▼ Submit ──► Assignments decide who gets it (category override → department's auto-assignee) ──► Ticket Queue

What's automatic vs. what you choose

The member only ever picks a Department and a Category. They never choose a staff member to route to — the Assignments screen decides that behind the scenes. Everything the member sees on the form is something you turned on in these screens.

2. The order to set things up in

Set the tabs up in this order. Each step builds on the one before it, so doing them out of order makes things harder.

1
Departments
Create the teams (Events, Billing, Membership, Technical…). This is the first thing the member picks.
2
Categories
Under each department, create the topics (e.g. Events → Points, Results, Staff). This is the second thing the member picks.
3
Staff & Assignments
Add your support staff, then use the Assignments screen to pick who works each department and who new tickets are automatically assigned to.
4
Custom Fields
Add the extra questions that appear for specific categories — including the special "Related Event", "My Purchase", and "Staff Member" fields.
5
Settings
Turn on auto-close for inactive tickets and other global options.

3. Step 1 — Departments (the teams)

Support Tickets → Settings → Departments

A department is a team that handles a kind of request. It is the first thing the member chooses on the form. Examples: General Support, Membership Services, Event Operations, Billing, Score & Points, Event Disputes.

1
Open the Departments tab
You'll see your existing departments listed.
2
Click "Add Department" (or edit an existing one)
Give it a clear name the member will understand (e.g. "Events"). Add a short description if helpful.
3
Set one department as the Default
If a ticket ever can't be matched to a team, it goes to the default. "General Support" is a good default.
4
Save
The department now appears in the first dropdown on the support form.

Tip

Keep the number of departments small and obvious to members (5–8 is plenty). You can have lots of categories underneath each one — that's where detail belongs.

4. Step 2 — Categories (the topics under each department)

Support Tickets → Settings → Categories

A category is a topic that lives inside one department. After the member picks a department, the form shows only that department's categories. Example: under the Events department you might create Points, Results, and Staff.

1
Open the Categories tab and click "Add Category"
2
Fill in the fields
  • Label — what the member sees (e.g. "Points").
  • Key — auto-fills from the label (e.g. points). It's the behind-the-scenes name. Leave it as suggested. It can't be changed after you save.
  • Department — choose which department this topic belongs to (e.g. Events).
  • Display order — lower numbers show first.
  • Active — leave ticked so it appears on the form.
3
Save
Repeat for every topic under each department.

Why the "Key" matters

The Key is how Custom Fields and Routing rules find this category. When you build a custom field later, you'll tick which categories it appears under — those use the Key. So pick a sensible label up front; the key follows it.

5. Step 3 — Staff & Assignments (who receives each ticket)

Support Tickets → Staff  then  Support Tickets → Settings → Assignments

First add your support people on the Staff tab (and tick "can be assigned tickets"). Then open Settings → Assignments — this one screen controls who works each department and who new tickets are automatically assigned to. Every change saves immediately (you'll see a little checkmark).

1
Add staff (Staff tab)
Add each support team member and make sure "can be assigned tickets" is on for anyone who should receive tickets.
2
Pick the staff for each department
On Assignments, each department has a "Support staff for this department" multi-select. Check everyone who works that team's tickets.
3
Set "Auto-assign new tickets to"
Choose the one person who should automatically receive every new ticket in that department. If a department shows a yellow "No auto-assignee" badge, its tickets will sit unassigned until someone claims them.
4
Optional: per-category overrides
Under each department, every category has its own dropdown. Leave it on "Department default (…)" to use the department's auto-assignee — the name in parentheses shows exactly who that is. Pick a specific person only when that one topic should go to someone else (e.g. "Bug report" → your web person).

How a new ticket finds its owner

When a ticket is submitted, the system assigns it in this order — first match wins:

  • 1. Routing rule — only if an advanced routing rule (below) matches AND names a staff member.
  • 2. Category override — the category's dropdown on the Assignments screen.
  • 3. Department auto-assignee — the department's "Auto-assign new tickets to" person.

If none of those are set, the ticket stays unassigned in the queue.

Heads up: "Production Staff Setup"

The owner-only Production Staff Setup → Apply setup button (on Settings → General) writes a fixed, pre-programmed staff list and department assignees. Running it will overwrite whatever you've configured on the Assignments screen — so once you manage assignments here, don't re-run Apply setup.

Advanced: Routing rules (keywords & priority escalation)

Support Tickets → Settings → Routing

You normally don't need this screen — the Assignments screen covers day-to-day "who gets what". Routing rules are advanced overrides that fire only when their conditions match, e.g. "if the description contains fraud, set priority to Critical" or "tickets matching these keywords go straight to a specific person". A rule that names a staff member beats the Assignments settings (that's what makes it an override). Higher-priority rules are checked first; the first matching rule wins. Use the Test button to preview where a sample ticket would land. If an old rule shows "(inactive)" or "Deleted staff member" next to its assignment, it's a leftover from an earlier setup — delete it.

6. Step 4 — Custom Fields (extra questions)

Support Tickets → Settings → Custom Fields

Custom fields are the extra questions that appear on the form based on the category the member picked. This is how you collect exactly the info your team needs for each kind of request.

Creating a field

1
Click "Add Field"
2
Fill in the basics
  • Label — the question the member sees (e.g. "Which class?").
  • Field key — auto-fills; leave it. Can't change after saving.
  • Field type — see the table below.
  • Help text — optional hint shown under the field.
3
Choose "Shown for categories"
Tick every category this field should appear under. (A field can appear under several.)
4
Set the toggles
  • Required (when shown) — the member must answer it (only enforced while the field is actually visible).
  • Visible to submitter — leave on for member-facing fields; turn off for internal/admin-only fields.
  • Active — leave on to use it.
5
Save
Pick the matching category on the live form to see your field appear.

The field types

TypeWhat it doesGood for
Single-line textA short text box.Order number, name, short answer.
Paragraph textA larger multi-line box.Longer explanations.
DropdownPick one option from a list you define."Issue type", "Which class".
Multi-selectPick several options."Which days affected".
CheckboxA single yes/no tick box."I've read the policy".
NumberA numbers-only box.Amount, quantity.
DateA date picker."Date of the issue".
Related EventA dropdown of events. The chosen event links to the ticket.Anything about a specific event.
My PurchaseShows the member their own purchases (memberships, shop orders, registrations) with refund eligibility. See refunds →Refund / billing requests.

Dropdown & multi-select need options

If you pick "Dropdown" or "Multi-select", an Options section appears. Add each choice (type the label; the value fills in for you). You need at least one option before you can save.

7. Step 5 — Conditional fields (show a field only when…)

This is the most powerful part. Any field can be set to appear only when another field has a certain answer. A hidden field is never required, so members only ever see what's relevant to them.

1
Edit the field you want to hide/show
e.g. a "Refund amount" field.
2
Tick "Only show this field when another field matches"
3
Choose the controlling field, the operator, and the value
  • Controlling field — another dropdown / checkbox / multi-select in the same category.
  • Operator:
  •   • is — show when the other field equals a specific value.
  •   • is one of — show when it matches any of several values.
  •   • is checked — show when a checkbox is ticked.
  •   • has any value — show as soon as the other field is filled in.
  • Value(s) — pick which answer(s) trigger it.
4
Save
The field now appears only when the rule is satisfied — live, as the member fills the form.

Example

Make an "Issue type" dropdown with options Refund, Question, Other. Then add a "My Purchase" field and set it to "Only show when Issue type is Refund." Now the purchase list only appears when someone is actually asking for a refund.

Order matters for conditions

The controlling field must already exist and share a category with the field you're hiding. Create the controlling field (the dropdown/checkbox) first, then create the field that depends on it.

8. Step 6 — Refund requests (the "My Purchase" field & refund panel)

The My Purchase field type turns the support form into a refund-request tool, and gives your staff a one-click refund button right on the ticket.

What the member sees

When you add a My Purchase field to a category, a logged-in member sees a list of their own purchases — memberships, shop orders, event registrations, and World Finals pre-registrations — each showing the amount, date, and a badge:

  • "Within 30-day refund window" (green) — eligible per policy.
  • "Outside 30-day refund window" (amber) — past policy. The member still sees a note linking to the Refund Policy, and can still submit — your team decides what to do.

What staff see (the refund panel)

On the ticket detail page, staff see a Referenced Purchase panel showing the item, purchase date, amount, payment method (Stripe/PayPal), transaction id, and the eligibility badge. There is an Issue Refund button:

1
Click "Issue Refund"
2
Choose full or partial
Tick "Partial refund" and enter a dollar amount for a partial; leave it unticked for a full refund.
3
Enter a reason and confirm
The refund is processed through the original payment provider (Stripe or PayPal) and the record is updated.

Important: older PayPal purchases

PayPal refunds work automatically only for purchases made after this system was deployed (when the PayPal reference started being saved). For an older PayPal purchase, the panel will tell you to issue the refund in the PayPal dashboard instead. All Stripe purchases — old or new — refund inline.

9. Step 7 — The "Staff Member" picker

The Staff Member field type shows the member a dropdown of your support/staff team. Use it for categories like "Staff" where a member's request is about a particular team member.

1
Make sure your team is on the Staff tab
Only active staff with "can be assigned tickets" appear in the picker.
2
Add a "Staff Member" custom field to the right category
e.g. attach it to "Staff" under the Events department, and set it to show only when needed (see conditional fields).

10. Step 8 — Auto-close inactive tickets

Support Tickets → Settings → General → Automation

The Auto-Close Inactive Tickets setting closes tickets that are waiting on the member and never get a reply, so your queue stays clean.

1
Set "days awaiting customer reply"
Enter the number of days, e.g. 2. Set it to 0 to turn the feature off.
2
What happens
  • When staff have replied but the member hasn't responded for that many days, the member gets an email: "…will automatically close within 24 hours."
  • If they still don't reply, the ticket auto-closes ~24 hours later.
  • If the member replies at any point, the timer resets — it won't close.
3
Click "Save Changes"

11. Full worked example: Events → Points / Results / Staff

Here's how to build the example flow end to end. Follow it once and the rest will click.

1
Department
On the Departments tab, make sure you have an Events department.
2
Categories
On the Categories tab, add three categories under Events: Points, Results, and Staff.
3
"Related Event" field for Points & Results
On Custom Fields, add (or edit the existing) Related Event field. Tick the categories Points and Results. Mark it Required. Now picking Points or Results shows the event picker.
4
"Staff Member" field for Staff
Add a Staff Member field and tick only the Staff category. Now picking "Staff" shows the team dropdown.
5
Assignments
On Assignments, set the Events department's staff and auto-assignee, then use the category overrides: Points/Results → your scorer; Staff → a manager.
6
Test it
Open the public support form, pick Events → Points, and confirm the Related Event field appears and is required. Switch to Staff and confirm the team dropdown appears instead.

12. Tips, gotchas & FAQ

"I changed a setting but the form didn't update."

Reload the support form page. The form fetches departments, categories, and fields fresh each time it opens.

"A field isn't showing up."

Check three things: (1) the field is Active, (2) Visible to submitter is on, and (3) it's ticked for the category the member selected. If it has a "show only when…" rule, make sure the controlling field's answer matches.

"Can guests use these fields?"

Yes — the Department → Category → custom field flow works on the public guest form too. The only exception is the My Purchase field, which needs a logged-in member account to list purchases.

"Do I have to make Category or Department required?"

They already are — the member must choose a department and then a category before they can submit.

Rule of thumb

Departments = a handful of broad teams. Categories = the specific topics under each. Custom fields = the exact details you need for each topic. Conditional rules = only ask for a detail when it's relevant.
