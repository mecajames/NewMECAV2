🏆 Competition Results Guide

Understanding your scores, placements, points, and achievement badges

Results Table

The Results tab displays all your competition results in a table format:

The results table with gold, silver, and bronze placement badges

ColumnDescription
EventThe name and date of the competition
FormatCompetition format (SPL, SQL, Show & Shine, Ride the Light)
ClassThe class you competed in
ScoreYour competition score (dB for SPL/SQL, points for Show & Shine)
PlaceYour placement with badge (Gold, Silver, Bronze for top 3)
PointsCompetition points earned from this placement

Placement Badges

Top finishers receive colored badges next to their placement:

  • 1st Place — Gold badge
  • 2nd Place — Silver badge
  • 3rd Place — Bronze badge

Placements below 3rd are shown without a colored badge.

Points System

MECA awards competition points based on your placement at each event. Points accumulate throughout the season and determine rankings.

Championship Events (4X)

Championship-level events (like the MECA Finals) use a 4X multiplier and may award extended placement points beyond 5th place.

Points are per-class, per-format. If you compete in multiple formats at the same event, you earn points for each one separately. Points from all events in a season accumulate toward your season total.
💡 Tip: For detailed point configuration for the current season, check the Points Configuration Guide.

Using Filters

Filter your results to focus on specific data:

  • Season — View results from a specific competition season
  • State — Filter by event state/location
  • Format — Show only SPL, SQL, Show & Shine, or Ride the Light results

Rating Event Staff

After competing at an event, you may be asked to rate the event staff (judges and event director). This helps MECA maintain quality standards:

  • Rating prompts appear after results are published
  • Rate on a 1-5 star scale
  • Optionally leave a comment
  • Ratings are anonymous — staff won't see who rated them

Disputing a Result

If you believe a result is incorrect:

1
First, contact the event director directly if possible — many issues can be resolved quickly.
2
If unresolved, submit a Support Ticket from Member Support.
3
Include:
  • Event name and date
  • Your competition class and format
  • The specific issue (wrong score, wrong class, missing result, etc.)
  • Any supporting evidence (photos, scorecard, etc.)
4
MECA will review your dispute and respond within a few business days.
⚠ Important: Disputes should be filed as soon as possible after results are posted. The sooner you report an issue, the easier it is to resolve while event data is still fresh.
