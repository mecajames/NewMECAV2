1. What Is a Secondary Membership?

A secondary membership lets you (the primary, or “master,” member) add additional competitor memberships under your own account. It’s the right choice when you want to cover:

  • Another person — a spouse, child, or friend who competes but whose membership you want to manage and pay for.
  • Another vehicle of your own — each vehicle that competes needs its own MECA ID.

Each secondary gets its own unique MECA ID, and you manage and pay for all of them from your single account.

🏎 Every unique vehicle gets its own MECA ID. If you want to run more than one vehicle — or add another driver — you add a secondary membership for each one.

2. Secondary Membership vs. Team

These are two different features — it’s easy to mix them up:

Secondary Membership Team
Additional individual memberships you own and pay for, each with its own MECA ID. A team of members with a shared name, roster, and team standings.
Managed from Membership & Billing. Managed from the Teams tab.
💡 Tip: If you’re looking to create or join a competition team, use the Teams tab instead — see the Team Management guide in the Help Center.

3. Before You Start

  • You need your own active membership first — secondaries are added underneath it.
  • Secondary memberships are Competitor memberships.
  • Have the competitor’s name and the vehicle details ready (make, model, color, and license plate).
⚠ Adding a second membership from the public site won’t work. If an email already has an active membership, the checkout will tell you so and ask you to log in. That’s by design — additional memberships are added here, as secondaries, from inside your account.

4. How to Add a Secondary Membership

Go to My MECA → the Membership & Billing tab, then find the Secondary Memberships section and click “Add Secondary Member.” A short wizard walks you through four steps:

1
Details. Choose “Who is this membership for?” (yourself, a family member, a friend, etc.). If it’s for you, we use your own name automatically; otherwise enter the competitor’s name. Then enter the vehicle information — make, model, color, and license plate (all required, since each unique vehicle earns its own MECA ID).
2
Account login option. Pick one:
  • No separate login — you manage this membership entirely from your account; the secondary can’t log in on their own.
  • Give their own login — they can log in, compete, and edit their profile. You still handle all billing. You’ll enter their email, and login details are sent there.
3
Membership type. Choose a Competitor membership type. The price is shown next to each option.
4
Review & confirm. Check the relationship, competitor, vehicle, login choice, and membership, then click “Add Secondary.” The secondary is created with a Pending payment status.
💡 Tip: Adding a secondary creates the membership but doesn’t charge you yet — it stays Pending until you complete payment (next section).

5. Paying for a Secondary Membership

A new secondary starts in a Pending state. To activate it, complete payment for that secondary from the Secondary Memberships section under Membership & Billing.

Once payment is confirmed, the secondary’s MECA ID becomes active and can be used to register and compete at events.

6. Managing & Editing a Secondary

All of your secondaries are listed in the Secondary Memberships section of the Membership & Billing tab, showing the count and each member’s details.

Editing details

Click the Edit (pencil) icon next to a secondary to update:

  • Competitor name
  • Relationship to you
  • Vehicle make, model, color, and license plate

Who can log in?

If you gave a secondary their own login, they sign in with their own email to manage their profile and compete — but billing always stays with you, the master account. If you chose no separate login, you manage everything for them from your account.

💡 Tip: Keep the vehicle details accurate — they’re tied to the MECA ID and used at event check-in.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

How many secondaries can I add?

As many as you need. Each one is its own membership with its own MECA ID.

What membership types can a secondary be?

Secondaries are Competitor memberships. Retailer/Manufacturer types and team add-ons aren’t available as secondaries.

Who pays for a secondary?

You do — the master account always handles billing, even when the secondary has their own login.

Is a secondary membership the same as a team?

No. A secondary is an additional individual membership under your account; a team is a separate feature with its own roster and standings. See section 2 above.

Why can’t I just buy another membership on the website?

If an email already has an active membership, the public checkout will stop you and ask you to log in. Additional memberships are added as secondaries here, inside your account, so everything stays under one login and one bill.
