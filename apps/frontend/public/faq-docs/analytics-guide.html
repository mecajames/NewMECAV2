5. Understanding the Charts

The analytics charts: placement distribution, score trends, and points by format

Upload as: analytics-charts.png • Title: Analytics Charts • Desc: Three analytics charts — placement distribution pie chart, score trends line chart, and points by format bar chart • Tags: faq, analytics, charts, pie, trends, bar

Placement Distribution (Pie Chart)

The pie chart shows the proportion of your finishes at each position. A larger slice for "1st Place" means you're winning more often. This visual makes it easy to spot if you're consistently podium-finishing or if there's room to improve.

Score Trends (Line Chart)

The line chart plots your scores over time, showing whether you're improving, holding steady, or declining. Look for:

  • Upward trend — Your scores are improving over time
  • Flat line — Consistent performance — you're reliable but may have hit a plateau
  • Downward trend — Scores declining — might indicate equipment issues or increased competition

Points by Format (Bar Chart)

The bar chart compares your total points across formats side by side. This makes it easy to see your strongest and weakest formats at a glance.

6. Using Filters

Use the season filter to compare your performance across different years:

  • Select a specific season to see stats for just that year
  • Select "All Seasons" to see your career totals
  • Compare two seasons side by side by switching between them and noting the numbers
💡 Tip: Comparing season-over-season stats is a great way to measure your improvement. Are you earning more points this year than last? Is your win rate going up?

7. Tips for Improving Your Performance

Use your analytics data to identify areas for growth:

Analyze Your Score Trends

  • If scores are flat, consider upgrading equipment or fine-tuning your setup
  • If scores dropped after a change, you may want to revert and investigate
  • Note which events produce your best scores — what's different about those venues?

Focus on Your Best Format

  • Your highest win rate format is where you're strongest — lean into it for maximum points
  • Consider competing in more events for your best format

Diversify for More Points

  • Competing in multiple formats at each event multiplies your point-earning opportunities
  • Even a 4th or 5th place finish earns points that add up over a season

Track Consistency

  • A high placement rate (lots of top-3 finishes) is often more valuable than occasional 1st places
  • Consistent top-5 finishes accumulate more season points than alternating between 1st and 10th

Compare Against Past Seasons

  • Use the season filter to track your year-over-year improvement
  • Set personal goals based on last season's numbers (e.g., "beat my total points by 20%")
Remember: The competition is with yourself first. Use analytics to track your personal growth, not just to compare with others. Every improvement in score, placement, or consistency is a win.
